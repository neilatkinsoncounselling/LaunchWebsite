Meet Neil

Neil Atkinson

Integrative Counsellor · MNCPS (Acc.) · NCPS Accredited Registrant

I came to counselling through real life rather than a textbook. My approach is warm, thoughtful and grounded. I work with you rather than applying a fixed method. My aim is to offer a space where you can talk openly, feel understood and begin to make sense of things in your own way.

What I help with

You do not need to have it all worked out. Let’s talk.

People come to counselling for many different reasons. You might recognise something here, or you might not. Both are completely fine.

Some of the things people come to me with:

  • Anxiety & Stress Racing thoughts, constant dread
  • Depression When everything feels flat and far away
  • Grief & Loss No right way, no timetable
  • Trauma You don’t have to carry it alone
  • Men’s Mental Health Asking for help is strength, not weakness
  • Neurodivergent Support A space for how your mind works

How I work

Counselling that fits your life

There is no single right way to start counselling. Choose the format that feels most manageable for you.

Some of the ways I work:

  • Face-to-Face Two people in a room, talking
  • Online Counselling From wherever you feel comfortable
  • Walk & Talk Side by side, outdoors. Moving helps.
  • Telephone Just your voice. No camera required.
See how I work

Starting counselling

How it works, step by step

  1. One

    Read a little and see how it feels

    Have a look around the site, read a little about me, and see whether the way I work feels right for you. You do not need to be certain before getting in touch.

    Read more about Neil

  2. Two

    Book your first free session

    A no pressure first conversation to talk about what has brought you here, ask questions, and see whether working together feels like a good fit.

    Read what actually happens in a first session.

  3. Three

    Begin at a pace that suits you

    If you decide to continue, we can begin regular sessions in person, online, or by phone, with space to work through things at your own pace.

  4. Four

    Review and continue thoughtfully

    Some people come for a few sessions and others for longer. We can review things as we go so the support stays useful, steady, and right for you.
