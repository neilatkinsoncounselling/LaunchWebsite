Privacy & confidentiality

Your privacy matters to me. This page explains in plain English how I handle your information, how your sessions are protected, and what your rights are. It also serves as my privacy notice under UK GDPR.

Data controller

The data controller for the personal information described on this page is Neil Atkinson, trading as Neil Atkinson Counselling. You can contact me at neil@neilatkinsoncounselling.co.uk.

I am registered with the Information Commissioner’s Office under registration reference ZC110402. My current data protection registration runs until 24 March 2027.

Your sessions are confidential

Everything you share in a session stays between us. I will not share your personal information with employers, family members, or anyone else without your explicit consent.

There are a small number of exceptions where I have a professional or legal duty to act. These are:

  • If I believe there is a serious and imminent risk of harm to you or to another person, including a child or vulnerable adult
  • If I am required to disclose information by a court order or by law, for example under the Terrorism Act 2000 or the Drug Trafficking Act 1994
  • If a safeguarding concern arises involving a child or vulnerable adult, in which case I may need to share relevant information with the appropriate safeguarding authority

In any of these situations, wherever possible I will discuss the matter with you first. These exceptions are rare, but I want you to understand them clearly from the outset. I will explain them again at our first session.

As part of my professional practice, I receive regular clinical supervision. This means I meet confidentially with an experienced supervisor to reflect on my work and make sure I am supporting you as effectively as possible. Your identity is never shared — only anonymised details may be discussed. Supervision is a professional requirement and one of the ways I maintain the quality and safety of my practice.

What information I collect and why

I collect only the information I need to provide you with a safe and effective counselling service. This includes:

  • Your name, contact details and emergency contact information
  • Brief clinical notes relating to your sessions
  • Any information you choose to share through the contact form on this website
  • Records relating to fees and payments

Lawful basis for processing

Under UK GDPR, I rely on the following lawful bases to process your personal data:

  • Legitimate interests — to respond to enquiries submitted through the contact form on this website, and to manage the practical aspects of our counselling relationship such as scheduling and payment
  • Contract — to provide the counselling service we have agreed to, including maintaining session notes and managing appointments
  • Explicit consent — for the processing of special category data, which includes information about your mental and physical health that you share during sessions. You may withdraw this consent at any time
  • Legal obligation — where I am required by law to retain certain records, such as financial records for tax purposes

How I store your data

All personal data is stored securely. Paper records are kept in a locked location. Digital records are stored on encrypted, password-protected devices. I take reasonable steps to protect your information from unauthorised access, loss or damage.

Some of your data may be processed by trusted service providers that I use to run my practice. These include Netlify for website hosting and contact form handling, Microsoft for email, and any secure video platform used for online sessions. I only use services that offer appropriate safeguards for your data, and I do not share your clinical or session notes with these providers unless it is necessary to provide the service, respond to your enquiry, or meet a legal or professional obligation.

How long I keep your data

I retain different types of information for different periods, in line with professional guidance and legal requirements:

  • Enquiries — if you contact me but do not go on to become a client, I will delete your details within six months of our last contact
  • Client records and session notes — retained for seven years after the end of our therapeutic relationship, in line with professional body guidance and insurance requirements
  • Financial records — retained for seven years as required by HMRC

After the relevant retention period, records are securely destroyed.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data I hold about you
  • Ask me to correct any inaccuracies in your data
  • Ask me to delete your data, where there is no overriding legal or professional reason for me to keep it
  • Withdraw your consent to the processing of special category data at any time
  • Object to data processing based on legitimate interests
  • Request that your data be transferred to another provider in a commonly used format

To exercise any of these rights, contact me at neil@neilatkinsoncounselling.co.uk. I will respond within one month.

If you are not satisfied with how I have handled your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO). You can contact the ICO at ico.org.uk or by telephone on 0303 123 1113.

Professional insurance

I hold professional liabilities insurance covering my counselling practice through Everywhen, underwritten by Markel International Insurance Company Limited. The current certificate is valid from 28 May 2026 to 27 May 2027 and provides a £1,000,000 limit of indemnity. Details are available on request.

NCPS membership and ethical framework

I am a member of the National Counselling and Psychotherapy Society (NCPS) and practise in accordance with their ethical framework. In practical terms, this means you can expect:

  • A qualified counsellor, MNCPS (Acc.), working to professional standards
  • Ongoing supervision and continued professional development
  • A clear complaints procedure if you are ever dissatisfied

You can read the full NCPS ethical framework at ncps.com.

Complaints

If you are unhappy with any aspect of my service, I would encourage you to raise it with me directly in the first instance. I will take your concern seriously and do my best to resolve it.

If you feel the matter has not been resolved, or if you would prefer not to raise it with me, you can make a formal complaint through the NCPS. Details of their complaints procedure are available at ncps.com.

This website and cookies

This website does not use tracking cookies or advertising technology. I do not profile visitors or share browsing data for advertising purposes. Technical server logs may be created by Netlify as part of normal website hosting. The only enquiry information I receive through this website is what you voluntarily submit via the contact form, and that is processed by Netlify and delivered to me through Microsoft email.

Any questions?

If you have any questions about how your data is handled or anything else on this page, please get in touch at neil@neilatkinsoncounselling.co.uk — I am happy to talk it through.
